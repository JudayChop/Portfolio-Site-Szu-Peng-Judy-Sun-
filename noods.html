About

Problem:

Popular recipe web apps today typically have recipes from all different cuisines. But there isn’t one that is specifically designed for one type of cuisine, in this case Asian cuisine. Personally, when I want to look for an Asian recipe, I don’t have a go-to place to look or one. The amount of search results are overwhelming and I wish there is a place where I can find all kinds of Asian cuisines and be able to trust the authenticity of the recipes.

Solution:

This is why I created Noods, an Asian recipe web app that is the hub for all types of Asian cuisine recipes. The goal is to offer recipes that are authentic and easy to follow. The app will allow users to browse through Asian recipes and be able to apply different filters to match their needs.

User Research

Goals:

To gather more knowledge and identify the needs of my potential users.

Methodology:

I used the process of the Research Learning Spiral and conducted my user research. I selected qualitative research, where I interviewed users to find out what their behaviors are with cooking and using recipe apps.

Results:

Users prefer recipes that are not time-consuming due to their busy lifestyles. They also want something that is easy to use with clear instructions and video/photo guidelines. And they also want to be able to customize their search while they look for a recipe.

Usability Test

Goals:

The goal of this test is to assess the learnability for users interacting with the recipe application for the first time on the mobile app. I would like to observe and measure if new users understand the project, its value, and how to complete basic initial functions such as logging in and searching for/navigating to a desired recipe.

Objectives:

  • How fast can people log in?
  • Can participants easily find search?
  • Do search results meet their expectation?
  • Can participants find specific type of recipe with ease?
  • Is the recipe page what they expected?

Mood Board

From the start I knew I wanted a color palette that has the color red in it because the color red is heavily used across Asian cultures. I wanted something that conveys authenticity while also keeping its uniqueness and fun. I found imagery and color schemes that matched what I was going for, along with icons, fonts, and other UI elements.

Challenges

One of the major challenges is that this was my first project where I had to learn the User Experience design process and also explore a more in-depth User Interface design process. Since all these design principles and processes were new to me, it was difficult to approach the project with full confidence. I took it slow and follow each step of the process one by one, and began to build a solid foundation.

Another challenge I encountered was defining my final design. When I was creating my high-fidelity wireframes, I realized I didn’t like how the original logo looked. I ended up creating a brand new logo while using my style guide for guidance. The new logo looked more polished and professional and matches better with my vision for the recipe app.

Takeaways

What I learned from this recipe app project:

  • UX and UI design principles and how to apply them to my design
  • Design can evolve throughout the designing process and that’s okay
  • Importance of user research and user testing so I can focus on the design problem while creating my design
  • Learning and utilizing different user testing methods (Usability test, A/B & preference test)
  • Recognizing responsive design patterns and how to define responsive grids
